Problem being solved
--------------------
- The repository already had concrete service exit observations in the real
  kernel artifact, but it still lacked one published aggregate proof that one
  service death becomes explicit and reviewable as a failure fact rather than
  disappearing into earlier service-handshake proofs.
- Without this step, later failure, IPC-composition, or device-boundary work
  could blur whether the current model already has an honest service-death
  observation answer or whether the kernel needs immediate supervision or
  restart machinery.
- The repository needed one small phase that classifies one service-death path
  through the existing bootstrap-service, wait-handle, init-owned directory,
  bootstrap-audit, and debug owners rather than through a new policy layer.

- Keep the current kernel owners and fixed service topology intact.

- Prove one bounded service death becomes visible through the current wait

- Do not widen into restart logic, service replacement, kernel supervision,
