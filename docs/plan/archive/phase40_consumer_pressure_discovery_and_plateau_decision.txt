Problem being solved
--------------------
- Several deferred fronts remain credible, but none is yet the obvious next
  active capability wave from admitted consumer pressure alone.
- Picking one prematurely would risk reopening a deferred family from abstract
  completeness arguments instead of from a real admitted need.
- The repository needs one explicit discovery phase that lands or re-audits a
  new pressure artifact and then routes the next series to the correct owner.

Core rule
---------
- Use one real pressure artifact to choose the next frontier.
- Do not commit to compiler, artifact, or stdlib widening before the artifact
  makes the owner-local gap concrete.
- Prefer admitted real projects or canonical refreshes over synthetic probes.
- Keep pressure honest: the new artifact should become a real repository proof
  surface, not a throwaway experiment.
- If the artifact does not expose a new frontier strongly enough, record that
  explicitly and stop rather than forcing a follow-on phase.

Exit criteria
