Phase 247 -- Parser Follow-Through For Chained Disjunctions And Trailing Commas
================================================================================

Status
------
PROPOSED

Purpose
-------
Admit one bounded parser follow-through phase that repairs two repeated source
shapes the language already wants to read naturally: chained ordinary `||`
disjunctions and trailing commas in ordinary comma-delimited expression or
aggregate lists.

This phase exists now because the repository already treats both surfaces as
reasonable source forms in planning, grammar, or nearby deferrals, but the
current parser still makes callers flatten simple disjunctions or hand-edit
comma lists more rigidly than the language needs. The next honest move is to
align the parser with that admitted source contract before opening deeper type
or literal waves.

Repository status note
----------------------
- `docs/arch/c-lang/language/syntax/grammar_spec.txt` already defines
  `logical_or_expr = logical_and_expr { "||" logical_and_expr }`, so chained
  disjunction is part of the concrete grammar contract rather than a new
  precedence design.
- `docs/plan/backlog.txt` records a real parser limitation where direct
  `a || b || c` conditions still produced statement-boundary parse errors in a
  Phase 242 scenario.
- Phase 192 deliberately left trailing commas in aggregate literals
  unsupported even after admitting expected-type brace aggregates.
- The repository still lacks one bounded parser phase whose primary purpose is
  to make repeated operator and list parsing match the admitted grammar more
  honestly.

Problem being solved
--------------------
- Ordinary boolean guards become noisier than they should be when callers must
  split a direct disjunction chain into multiple statements solely because the
  parser mishandles repeated `||`.
- Ordinary list editing becomes more brittle when callers cannot leave a final
  comma in already comma-delimited expression or aggregate lists.
- Both defects are parser-owned. They do not require new control-flow meaning,
  new type rules, or new runtime behavior; they require the concrete grammar
  and parser implementation to agree.

Non-goals
---------
- no new boolean operators or precedence redesign
- no change to short-circuit semantics
- no trailing separator admission in newline-separated statement blocks
- no hidden semicolon insertion
- no standalone empty collection literal syntax
- no broader aggregate-literal inference work

Invariants
----------
- `||` keeps its current precedence relative to `&&`, bitwise operators, and
  range expressions.
- Logical disjunction remains short-circuiting and must keep the existing MIR
  lowering shape rather than degrading to plain binary eager evaluation.
- Trailing commas are admitted only in list forms that are already comma-
  delimited.
- Newline-separated statement and block boundaries remain explicit and
  unchanged.
- Parser determinism and diagnostic locality must remain intact.

Required surface changes
------------------------
1. Make parser handling of `logical_or_expr` accept direct chains such as
   `a || b || c` in ordinary condition and expression positions.
2. Admit trailing commas in the comma-delimited expression and aggregate list
   forms that the current grammar already owns, including aggregate-init field
   lists and ordinary expression-list owners such as calls, returns, bindings,
   and assignments.
3. Keep diagnostics explicit when a comma or disjunction is still malformed;
   do not trade honest parse errors for silent token skipping.
4. Add focused regressions for chained `||` parsing plus short-circuit
   follow-through and for trailing commas in the admitted list positions.
5. Spend the repaired source forms in at least one real repository-owned file
   or fixture that currently carries avoidable parser workarounds.

Forbidden changes
-----------------
- do not widen into new operator classes, associativity redesign, or boolean
  algebra features beyond repeated `||`
- do not admit trailing commas in statement sequences, switch case groups, or
  other newline-owned separators that are not already comma lists
- do not paper over parser defects by restructuring owner code instead of
  fixing the parser
- do not change MIR short-circuit lowering contracts merely to accommodate the
  parse repair

Phase role in the larger sequence
---------------------------------
- This phase follows the local-target ergonomics work by repairing the next
  narrowest parser-owned source distortions.
- It consumes one grammar or backlog mismatch around repeated disjunction and
  one long-deferred list-editing gap from the aggregate-literal line.
- It should leave later array-type and empty-literal phases working over a
  parser that already handles ordinary repeated operator and comma-list forms
  honestly.

Relevant backlog items this phase intentionally brings under pressure
--------------------------------------------------------------------
- consider reliable parsing of chained boolean `||` disjunctions in ordinary
  condition expressions
- aggregate literal list editing pressure that still lacks trailing commas
  after Phase 192

Questions this phase must answer
--------------------------------
1. Where is the exact parser control path that still truncates or mis-binds a
   repeated `||` chain despite the grammar contract already admitting it?
2. Which comma-delimited list owners can share one trailing-comma repair
   without reopening statement-boundary or newline-separator rules?
3. Does chained `||` need any AST or MIR follow-through beyond the parse fix,
   or is the controlling defect entirely in parser construction?
4. Can the repository spend both repairs immediately in one or two real caller
   shapes instead of keeping them as synthetic grammar-only wins?

Preferred implementation shape
------------------------------
- Keep the controlling ownership in `compiler/parse` and its nearby AST shape.
- Reuse the existing precedence ladder and repeated-list parsing helpers where
  practical rather than adding special one-off branches.
- Keep trailing-comma support explicit per list owner so the admitted surface
  stays reviewable.
- Reuse the existing logical short-circuit lowering contract once parser and
  AST construction preserve the full chained disjunction shape.
- Update the concrete grammar documents if the admitted list surface changes
  beyond the currently written aggregate-init form.

Test obligations
----------------
- `Build debug`
- one focused parser or sema regression for chained `a || b || c`
- one focused MIR or executable regression proving chained `||` keeps honest
  short-circuit behavior through lowering
- focused parser regressions for trailing commas in admitted comma-delimited
  list forms
- the narrowest owning parser, sema, and MIR or executable test targets for
  the touched source forms

Acceptance criteria
-------------------
- direct chained `||` expressions parse and lower without statement-boundary
  failures or eager-evaluation regressions
- trailing commas work in the admitted comma-delimited list positions and do
  not leak into newline-owned statement separators
- the landing stays parser-scoped rather than widening into unrelated type,
  literal, or control-flow redesign
- at least one real repository-owned caller or fixture becomes simpler because
  the parser now accepts the natural source form directly

Future follow-up
----------------
- If later list-editing pressure reaches other punctuation families, treat
  those as separate parser phases rather than smuggling them into this one.
- If a later phase needs standalone empty-literal syntax, keep that work in an
  expected-type literal phase rather than coupling it back to trailing commas.
- If chained boolean pressure later reaches `&&` and `||` diagnostics or AST
  formatting quality, record that as bounded follow-through after this repair
  lands.
