Phase 215 -- Queue Pressure And Backpressure First Probe
=======================================================

Status
------
PROPOSED

Purpose
-------
Move the running-system story from inspection into pressure by defining one
honest bounded queue-pressure and backpressure contract, including what
happens when an admitted queue-owning path reaches capacity and what visible
consequence callers observe.

This phase is real implementation work.
It is not a proof-only or documentation-only exercise.
It should add one narrow pressure behavior over an already-admitted queue
owner rather than reopening scheduler, mailbox, or concurrency frameworks.

Repository status note
----------------------
- The running-system slice already has a retained queue owner and bounded queue
  observability through count and peek.
- Current phases have made restart, retained state, policy, authority, and
  service-state inspection more explicit.
- What the system still does not answer clearly is what happens under queue
  pressure: when a queue is full, which bounded policy applies, and how that
  truth becomes visible to callers.

Problem being solved
--------------------
- Current queue semantics describe capacity and observation, but not yet one
  explicit bounded pressure contract.
- Without one pressure phase, future work on service stalls, timeouts, or
  richer execution behavior risks being built on an unspecified scarcity model.
- The missing truth is narrow: when one admitted queue reaches capacity, does
  the system reject, drop, or defer, and how does that show up to callers and
  inspection surfaces.

Core rule
---------
- Apply pressure to one real owner before widening execution semantics.
- Keep queue-pressure behavior explicit, deterministic, and owner-local.
- Prefer one bounded caller-visible consequence over generic backpressure
  infrastructure.
- Do not introduce scheduler frameworks, blocking queues, subscriptions, or
  mailbox systems.

Chosen slice for this phase
---------------------------
1. choose one admitted queue-owning path as the pressure owner
2. define one explicit full-queue policy such as reject or bounded drop,
   whichever is the narrowest honest policy for that owner
3. expose the caller-visible consequence through the existing bounded protocol
   and, if appropriate, the system-introspection surface
4. add one deterministic scenario and one focused reset-lane fixture proving
   full-queue behavior and its visible consequence
5. make no compiler, sema, MIR, backend, ABI, target, runtime, stdlib, or
   `hal` expansion beyond the narrow pressure contract

Planned outcome
---------------
- the running system gains its first explicit scarcity or backpressure truth
- queue-owning services no longer rely on implicit capacity behavior
- later stall and timeout work can refer to a real bounded pressure model
  rather than an unspecified queue-full condition

Preferred implementation shape
------------------------------
Owner path
- Keep the chosen queue owner responsible for full-queue semantics.
- Keep policy local to that owner and its direct protocol consequences.

Policy path
- Prefer one of the current narrow policy classes:
  - explicit reject with visible failure
  - explicit bounded drop with visible observation
- Avoid retry loops, blocking semantics, or general buffering layers.

Inspection path
- If queue pressure should surface in system introspection, report only a small
  bounded consequence marker or count-derived truth.
- Do not widen into deep queue statistics or historical telemetry.

Suggested execution order
-------------------------
1. Choose the narrowest honest queue owner and full-capacity policy.
2. Implement the policy in the owner-local service path.
3. Thread the caller-visible consequence through the existing protocol.
4. Add one deterministic scenario that drives the queue to capacity and proves
   the chosen policy.
5. Add one focused reset-lane fixture and any narrow inspection update needed
   to keep the policy visible.

Deliverables
------------
- one explicit bounded queue-pressure policy for one admitted queue owner
- one caller-visible consequence path for the chosen policy
- one scenario proof and one reset-lane fixture for full-queue behavior
- bounded inspection-surface follow-through only if needed for honesty

Validation expectations
-----------------------
- the tree must continue to build after the queue-pressure behavior lands
- the focused queue-pressure fixture must pass
- existing queue observability, retained-summary, retained-policy, and service-
  state introspection fixtures must remain green unless expected outputs
  intentionally widen

Exit criteria
-------------
- at least one admitted queue-owning path has an explicit deterministic full-
  capacity policy
- callers can observe the consequence of that policy through an existing
  bounded protocol or inspection surface
- the behavior is proved in deterministic fixture coverage
- no scheduler, blocking-queue, mailbox, or generic backpressure framework is
  introduced

Non-goals
---------
- general scheduler policy
- blocking dequeue or blocking send semantics
- mailbox or subscription systems
- queue history, telemetry, or large statistical surfaces
- multi-owner global pressure coordination

Why this is the narrowest honest landing
---------------------------------------
- The repo now has enough inspection and retained clarity that a first real
  scarcity contract will teach more than another naming-only phase.
- Queue pressure is a real local owner problem and does not require reopening
  execution or concurrency frameworks yet.
- One bounded policy gives later stall and timeout phases a concrete base.

Acceptance criteria for this document
-------------------------------------
- Phase 215 is explicitly framed as one owner-local queue-pressure probe, not
  as a general backpressure or scheduler phase.
- The phase keeps pressure deterministic, protocol-visible, and tightly
  bounded.
